# **Lesson 1: Exploring Product Owner / Product Management**

## **1.1 Introducing SAFe for Product Owners and Product Management**

### **Concept: The Product Owner (PO)**

**SAFe Level:** Team

#### **Definition**

The Product Owner is the member of the Agile Team responsible for defining Stories and prioritizing the Team Backlog to streamline the execution of program priorities while maintaining the conceptual and technical integrity of the Features or components for the team.

#### **Responsibilities**

1. **Connecting with the Customer:**  
   * Know the Customer and stakeholders.  
   * Identify the problem to be solved.  
   * Develop whole-product solutions.  
2. **Contributing to the Vision and Roadmap:**  
   * Understand market forces.  
   * Represent the end user.  
   * Assist with ART Backlog prioritization.  
   * Educate the ART during PI Planning.  
3. **Managing and Prioritizing the Team Backlog:**  
   * Guide Story creation.  
   * Prioritize backlog items.  
   * Accept Stories.  
   * Support Architectural Runway.  
4. **Supporting the Team in Delivering Value:**  
   * Balance stakeholder perspectives.  
   * Elaborate Stories.  
   * Foster Built-in Quality.  
   * Participate in team and ART events.  
5. **Getting and Applying Feedback:**  
   * Test benefit hypotheses.  
   * Obtain feedback from Customers and stakeholders.  
   * Share feedback with the ART.  
   * Evolve Solution design.

### **Concept: Product Management (PM)**

**SAFe Level:** Program / ART

#### **Definition**

Product Management represents the internal voice of the Customer and works with customers and Product Owners to understand and communicate their needs, define system features, and participate in validating system behavior.

#### **Responsibilities**

1. **Exploring Markets and Users:** Conducting market research and understanding user needs.  
2. **Connecting with the Customer:** Building relationships and feedback loops.  
3. **Defining Product Strategy, Vision, and Roadmaps:** Setting the long-term direction.  
4. **Managing and Prioritizing the ART Backlog:** Owning the flow of Features.  
5. **Delivering Value:** ensuring the solution provides economic benefit.

### **Diagram: PO/PM Content Authority**

* **Product Management (Program Level):**  
  * **Backlog:** ART Backlog.  
  * **Content Authority:** Vision, Roadmap, Features, PI Objectives.  
  * **Collaboration:** Works with System Architect and Business Owners.  
* **Product Owner (Team Level):**  
  * **Backlog:** Team Backlog.  
  * **Content Authority:** Iteration Goals, Stories, Story Acceptance.  
  * **Collaboration:** Works with System Architect (on Enablers) and the Agile Team.

## **1.2 The Lean-Agile Mindset**

### **Concept: The Agile Manifesto**

The foundation of Agile development, valuing:

* **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools.  
* **Working software** over comprehensive documentation.  
* **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation.  
* **Responding to change** over following a plan.

### **Concept: SAFe Lean-Agile Principles**

Ten principles that organize the SAFe framework:

1. **Take an economic view.**  
2. **Apply systems thinking.**  
3. **Assume variability; preserve options.**  
4. **Build incrementally with fast, integrated learning cycles.**  
5. **Base Milestones on objective evaluation of working systems.**  
6. **Make value flow without interruptions.**  
7. **Apply cadence, synchronize with cross-domain planning.**  
8. **Unlock the intrinsic motivation of knowledge workers.**  
9. **Decentralize decision-making.**  
10. **Organize around value.**

## **1.3 Value Streams**

### **Concept: Operational Value Streams**

**SAFe Level:** Portfolio

#### **Definition**

The sequence of activities needed to deliver a product or service to a Customer.

#### **Examples**

* Manufacturing a product.  
* Fulfilling an e-commerce order.  
* Admitting and treating a patient.  
* Providing a loan.

### **Concept: Development Value Streams**

**SAFe Level:** Portfolio

#### **Definition**

The sequence of activities needed to convert a business hypothesis into a technology-enabled product or service that delivers Customer value. These value streams build the systems that *enable* Operational Value Streams.

#### **Structure**

* **Trigger:** Feature Request.  
* **Process:** Define \-\> Build \-\> Validate \-\> Release.  
* **Value:** New increment of value.

### **Concept: Agile Release Train (ART)**

**SAFe Level:** Program

#### **Definition**

A virtual organization of 5 to 12 teams (50 to 125 individuals) that plans, commits, and executes together.

#### **Key Characteristics**

* **Cross-functional:** Contains all capabilities (software, hardware, firmware, testing, compliance, etc.) needed to deliver value.  
* **Aligned:** Aligns to a common mission via the ART Backlog.  
* **Cadence:** Synchronizes on a common cadence (The Planning Interval).

# **Lesson 2: Preparing for PI Planning**

## **2.1 PI Planning**

### **Concept: Planning Interval (PI)**

**SAFe Level:** Program

#### **Definition**

A timebox (typically 8â€“12 weeks) during which an ART delivers incremental value in the form of working, tested software and systems. It consists of multiple development Iterations followed by one Innovation and Planning (IP) Iteration.

### **Concept: PI Planning Event**

#### **Definition**

A cadence-based event that serves as the heartbeat of the ART, aligning all teams on the ART to a shared mission and Vision.

#### **Inputs**

* **Vision & Top 10 Features:** Provided by Product Management.  
* **Roadmap:** Future forecast.  
* **NFRs:** Non-functional requirements.

#### **Outputs**

* **Committed PI Objectives:** A set of SMART objectives for each team and the ART.  
* **ART Planning Board:** Visualizing dependencies and risks.

#### **Roles Involved**

* **Product Management:** Owns Feature priorities, negotiates scope, contributes to business value scores.  
* **Agile Teams:** Own Story planning and high-level estimates.  
* **Architect/UX:** Work as intermediaries for governance, interfaces, and dependencies.

---

## **2.2 The Solution Vision**

### **Concept: Solution Vision**

#### **Definition**

A description of the future state of the product or Solution. It answers: "Where are we headed?"

#### **Components**

* **Problem Solved:** What user pain point does it address?  
* **Features:** What benefit hypotheses are provided?  
* **Audience:** For whom does it provide benefit?  
* **NFRs:** What performance, reliability, or platform requirements exist?

#### **Common Formats**

* Rolling wave briefings.  
* Vision document.  
* Preliminary data sheet.  
* Draft press release ("Postcard from the Future").

#### **Inputs**

* **Strategic Themes:** Portfolio-level business objectives that influence the Vision.  
* **Customer Feedback:** Direct input evolving the Vision.

---

## **2.3 Solution and PI Roadmaps**

### **Concept: Solution Roadmap**

**SAFe Level:** Large Solution / Portfolio

#### **Definition**

A long-term view (multi-year) showing key milestones and deliverables.

#### **Components**

* **Timeline:** Q1, Q2, Q3, etc.  
* **Deliverables:** Epics, Capabilities, major Milestones.

### **Concept: PI Roadmap**

**SAFe Level:** Program

#### **Definition**

A shorter-term view (1-3 PIs) with higher fidelity.

#### **Structure**

* **Current PI (Committed):** What we are building now.  
* **Next 2 PIs (Forecast):** Projected Features and Milestones.

### **Concept: Market Rhythms & Events**

#### **Market Rhythms**

Cyclical and predictable events (e.g., Holiday shopping, annual enrollment, fiscal year end). These define the **Optimal Release Window**.

#### **Market Events**

Ad-hoc and unpredictable (e.g., Competitor release, regulatory change, technology breakthrough). These require agility to respond.

---

## **2.4 Customer-centric Features**

### **Concept: Feature**

**SAFe Level:** Program

#### **Definition**

A service that fulfills a stakeholder need. Each Feature includes a name, a benefit hypothesis, and acceptance criteria. It must be sized to be delivered by a single ART in a single PI.

#### **Hierarchy**

1. **Epic:** Portfolio level container for significant initiatives.  
2. **Feature:** Program level service fulfilling a need.  
3. **Story:** Team level description of desired functionality.

#### **Tools for Creating Features**

1. **Personas:** Characterizations of the people who might use the product (e.g., "Mike the Fleet Manager").  
2. **Customer Journey Maps:** Visualization of the end-to-end Customer experience (Decide \-\> Learn \-\> Choose \-\> Apply \-\> Purchase \-\> Complete).  
3. **Whole-Product Thinking:**  
   * *Generic Product:* Minimum to satisfy.  
   * *Expected Product:* Typical features.  
   * *Augmented Product:* Differentiators.  
   * *Potential Product:* Future vision.

#### **Activity: Feature Refinement**

* **Description:** Clear statement of functionality.  
* **Benefit Hypothesis:** The proposed measurable benefit to the user or business.  
* **Acceptance Criteria:** Specific conditions that must be met for the Feature to be accepted.

## **2.5 ART Backlog and Kanban**

### **Concept: ART Backlog**

**SAFe Level:** Program

#### **Definition**

The holding area for upcoming Features and Enablers that will address user needs and deliver business benefits for a single ART.

#### **Roles**

* **Content Authority:** Product Management prioritizes the ART Backlog.  
* **Collaboration:** System Architects help prioritize Enablers.

### **Concept: Capacity Allocation**

#### **Definition**

A policy to balance different types of work within the backlog.

#### **Typical Categories**

* New Features (Business value).  
* Enablers (Architectural Runway).  
* Tech Debt / Maintenance.

#### **Why This Matters**

Prevents the "tyranny of the urgent" where immediate features crowd out necessary architectural work or maintenance, ensuring long-term system health.

### **Concept: WSJF (Weighted Shortest Job First)**

#### **Definition**

A prioritization model used to sequence jobs (e.g., Features, Capabilities, Epics) to produce maximum economic benefit.

#### **Formula**

$$WSJF \= \\frac{\\text{Cost of Delay (CoD)}}{\\text{Job Size (Duration)}}$$

#### **Components of Cost of Delay (CoD)**

1. **User-Business Value:** Relative value to the customer or business. Revenue impact? Penalty?  
2. **Time Criticality:** Is there a fixed deadline? Will they wait? Decay of value over time?  
3. **Risk Reduction / Opportunity Enablement (RR/OE):** Does this reduce future risk or enable new business opportunities?

#### **Calculation Logic**

1. Score each component of CoD using relative Fibonacci numbers (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 20...).  
2. Sum the three components to get Total CoD.  
3. Estimate Job Size (Duration) using relative numbers.  
4. Divide Total CoD by Job Size.  
5. Highest WSJF score is prioritized first.

# **Lesson 3: Leading PI Planning**

## **3.1 Communicate the Vision**

### **Activities during Day 1**

1. **Present Vision:** PM presents how the Vision aligns with Strategic Themes and Solution Context.  
2. **Present Roadmap:** Communicate the PI Roadmap to assist planning.  
3. **Top 10 Features:** Communicate the prioritized Features (no surprises; teams should have seen these in refinement).

---

## **3.2 Establish PI Objectives**

### **Concept: PI Objectives**

**SAFe Level:** Team & Program

#### **Definition**

A summary of the business and technical goals that each team and the overall ART intend to achieve in the upcoming PI.

#### **Why This Matters**

* **Immediate Feedback:** Confirms teams understand desired outcomes.  
* **Decentralized Decisions:** Allows teams to trade off specific Stories while still hitting the objective.  
* **Predictability:** Focuses on outcomes rather than output.

#### **Structure (SMART)**

* **S**pecific  
* **M**easurable  
* **A**chievable  
* **R**ealistic  
* **T**ime-bound

### **Concept: Uncommitted Objectives**

#### **Definition**

Planned objectives that are *not* included in the commitment but are part of the plan.

#### **When This Is Used**

* When a team has low confidence in meeting the objective.  
* When the objective has many unknowns (high risk).  
* Note: They *do* count toward capacity/load but *not* toward the reliability metric.

---

## **3.3 Manage Dependencies**

### **Concept: ART Planning Board**

#### **Definition**

A physical or digital visualization of the PI plan, highlighting Features and dependencies between teams.

#### **Visual Elements (Legend)**

* **Blue Card:** Feature.  
* **Red/Pink Card:** Significant Dependency.  
* **Orange Card:** Milestone or Event.  
* **Red String:** Connector showing a dependency between a Feature/Milestone and another item.

#### **Dependency Types**

* **Iteration Dependencies:** Item A must be finished before Item B in the next iteration.  
* **Unbalanced Teams:** One team has too much load; another has capacity.  
* **Complex Critical Path:** Multiple teams linked in a chain.

---

## **3.4 Manage Risks**

### **Concept: ROAMing Risks**

#### **Definition**

A categorization method used during the Final Plan Review to address ART PI Risks.

#### **Categories (ROAM)**

* **R \- Resolved:** The risk has been addressed; no longer a concern.  
* **O \- Owned:** Someone has taken responsibility to handle it.  
* **A \- Accepted:** Nothing more can be done; if it occurs, the release may be compromised.  
* **M \- Mitigated:** Team has a plan to adjust if the risk occurs.

### **Concept: Confidence Vote**

A "Fist of Five" vote taken by the teams and the ART to indicate confidence in the plan.

* **Commitment:** Teams agree to do everything in their power to meet objectives and escalate immediately if feasibility changes.

# **Lesson 4: Executing Iterations**

## **4.1 Stories and Story Maps**

### **Concept: User Stories**

**SAFe Level:** Team

#### **Definition**

Short descriptions of a small piece of desired functionality, written in the user's language.

#### **Format (User Voice)**

As a **(user role)**, I want **(activity)** so that **(business value)**.

### **Concept: Enabler Stories**

**SAFe Level:** Team

#### **Definition**

Stories that support exploration, architecture, infrastructure, and compliance. They build the "railway" for the User Stories.

### **Concept: The Three Cs**

1. **Card:** Written on a card/tool; holds the intent.  
2. **Conversation:** The details are worked out in conversation between PO and Team.  
3. **Confirmation:** Acceptance criteria confirm correctness.

### **Concept: INVEST**

Criteria for good Stories:

* **I**ndependent  
* **N**egotiable  
* **V**aluable  
* **E**stimable  
* **S**mall (Fits in one Iteration)  
* **T**estable

### **Concept: Splitting Stories**

Techniques to break large Stories/Features down:

1. Workflow steps.  
2. Business rule variations.  
3. Major effort.  
4. Simple/Complex.  
5. Variations in data.  
6. Data entry methods.  
7. Operations.  
8. Use case scenarios.  
9. Break out a spike.

---

## **4.2 Iteration Planning**

### **Concept: Iteration Planning Event**

#### **Purpose**

Refines the iteration plans created during PI Planning. It is a 4-hour (max) timebox.

#### **Agenda**

1. **Establish Capacity:** Adjust for time off, holidays, maintenance.  
2. **Story Analysis & Estimating:** Discuss details, size stories (relative points).  
3. **Detailing Stories:** Define tasks, acceptance criteria.  
4. **Develop Iteration Goals:** Summarize what will be achieved.  
5. **Commit:** Team commits to the goals.

### **Concept: Velocity**

The sum of Story points for all completed Stories in an iteration. Used to predict future capacity.

---

## **4.3 The Team Kanban**

### **Concept: Team Kanban**

A visual tool to monitor and manage workflow.

* **Columns:** To Do, Doing, Done (can be customized).  
* **WIP Limits:** Constraints on columns to promote flow (Work In Process).  
* **Policies:** Explicit rules for moving items (Definition of Done).

---

## **4.4 Backlog Refinement**

### **Definition**

A continuous process to preview and elaborate upcoming Stories.

* **Time:** 1 hour per week (typical).  
* **Goal:** Ensure the backlog is ready for the *next* Iteration Planning.

---

## **4.5 Iteration Review and Retrospective**

### **Concept: Iteration Review**

* **Purpose:** Demonstrate working software/hardware to stakeholders.  
* **Format:** Demo every Story, Spike, Refactor, and NFR.  
* **Outcome:** Feedback, accepted stories.

### **Concept: Iteration Retrospective**

* **Purpose:** Relentless improvement (Process focus).  
* **Format:** Quantitative (Did we meet goals?) and Qualitative (What went well/poorly?).  
* **Outcome:** Improvement Backlog Items.

---

## **4.6 DevOps and Release on Demand**

### **Concept: Continuous Delivery Pipeline (CDP)**

1. **Continuous Exploration:** Understand needs (Hypothesize, Research, Architect).  
2. **Continuous Integration:** Build and Test (Develop, Build, Test, Stage).  
3. **Continuous Deployment:** Deploy to production (Verify, Monitor, Respond).  
4. **Release on Demand:** Make available to users (Release, Stabilize, Measure, Learn).

### **Concept: Decoupling Deployment from Release**

* **Deployment:** Moving code to production environments (Technical act).  
* **Release:** Making functionality available to users (Business decision).

# **Lesson 5: Executing the PI**

## **5.1 The PO Sync**

### **Concept: PO Sync**

**SAFe Level:** Program

#### **Definition**

A sync event (usually weekly) for Product Owners and Product Management to align on progress and scope.

#### **Purpose**

* Provide visibility into ART progress toward PI Objectives.  
* Assess scope adjustments.  
* Prepare for next PI (backlog refinement, WSJF).

---

## **5.2 The System Demo**

### **Concept: System Demo**

**SAFe Level:** Program

#### **Definition**

A significant event where the *integrated* system (all teams' work combined) is demonstrated to stakeholders.

#### **Timing**

Occurs every iteration (typically 2 weeks), ideally after team demos.

#### **Attendees**

Business Owners, Product Management, POs, RTE, System Architect, Stakeholders.

---

## **5.3 The Innovation and Planning (IP) Iteration**

### **Definition**

The final iteration of the PI. It acts as an estimating guard band.

### **Functions**

1. **Innovation:** Hackathons, exploration.  
2. **Planning:** PI Planning event occurs here.  
3. **Estimating Guard Band:** Buffer for overflow work to prevent carrying debt into the next PI.  
4. **Education:** Continuous learning.

### **Warning**

"If you don't plan for the IP Iteration, you will not have one." (Work will expand to fill the time).

---

## **5.4 Inspect and Adapt (I\&A)**

### **Concept: Inspect and Adapt Event**

#### **Definition**

A significant event held at the end of the PI where the current state of the Solution is demonstrated and evaluated.

#### **Three Parts**

1. **PI System Demo:** Showing all Features delivered in the PI.  
2. **Quantitative and Qualitative Measurement:** Reviewing Metrics.  
3. **Retrospective and Problem-Solving Workshop:** Addressing systemic issues.

### **Concept: Metrics**

#### **Flow Metrics**

* **Flow Distribution:** Type of work (Feature vs Enabler vs Debt).  
* **Flow Velocity:** Items completed per timeframe.  
* **Flow Time:** Time to go through the system.  
* **Flow Load:** WIP.  
* **Flow Efficiency:** Value-added time vs. wait time.  
* **Flow Predictability:** Planned vs. Actual business value.

#### **Outcome Metrics**

* **KPIs:** Key Performance Indicators for the value stream.  
* **OKRs:** Objectives and Key Results.

### **Concept: Problem-Solving Workshop**

1. Agree on the problem.  
2. Apply Root Cause Analysis (Fishbone/5 Whys).  
3. Identify the biggest root cause (Pareto analysis).  
4. Brainstorm solutions.  
5. Identify improvement backlog items.

# **Glossary of Key Terms**

* **Agile Release Train (ART):** A long-lived team of Agile teams that incrementally develops, delivers, and where applicable operates, one or more solutions in a value stream.  
* **Built-In Quality:** Practices that ensure that each Solution element, at every increment, meets appropriate quality standards throughout development.  
* **CapEx/OpEx:** Capital Expenses and Operating Expenses.  
* **Enablers:** Stories, Features, or Epics that support the activities needed to extend the Architectural Runway to provide future business functionality.  
* **NFRs (Nonfunctional Requirements):** System qualities such as reliability, performance, maintainability, scalability, and usability.  
* **Solution Context:** The environment in which the Solution operates.  
* **Story Points:** A relative unit of size used to estimate the effort required to implement a User Story.  
* **Velocity:** A measure of a teamâ€™s rate of progress during an Iteration (total story points completed).  
* **WSJF:** Weighted Shortest Job First, a prioritization model.
